Suspension of disbelief

Wed Feb 29, 2012 by

You watch a movie. In order to get something from it, you have to enter the world that it presents. You must suspend disbelief. This is essential for the product to work correctly. If you are unwilling to believe, say, that space mercenaries have Wookiee friends, or that the bumbling Bluth family exists — then it’s very unlikely the product is for you. Restated, you know full well that none of what you perceive actually exists, but you believe it in order to get something out of the experience. Read more →
