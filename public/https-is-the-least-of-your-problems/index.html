HTTPS is the least of your problems

Its cool that the EFF is offering a Firefox extension to use SSL where its available. I prefer to encrypt things too, but really, thats the least of your problems.

As I am sure you know, HTTPS simply encrypts data between your computer and the web server, while its in transit. It does nothing to ensure that your data is handled correctly at the other end.

When you hear about credit card or other privacy breaches, its usually due to mishandlingdatabases that get into the wrong hands, or software flaws that reveal information. SSL cant help you there.

Using SSL by default is still a good idea. But its like locking the car doors on the way to drop off your kid at day care. Those doors locks are fine, but its the day care you need to trust.
